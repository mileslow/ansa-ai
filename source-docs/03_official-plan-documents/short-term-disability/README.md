## 01 - Yale University Salary Continuation Program

- **Title:** Your Benefit Program - Salary Continuation
- **Organization:** Yale University; claims-evaluation document produced for The Hartford
- **Jurisdiction/market:** Connecticut; large-employer higher-education workforce
- **Plan/program design:** Employer-sponsored salary continuation, effective January 1, 2026. Benefits begin on the eighth day. The plan pays 100% of pre-disability earnings for weeks 2-8 and 60% for weeks 9-26, subject to deductible income. Maximum payable duration is 25 weeks after the waiting period.
- **Document subtype:** Detailed employer salary-continuation program document; explicitly states that it is not an ERISA employee welfare benefit plan
- **Why distinct:** Demonstrates a self-funded/employer-responsibility model and a stepped benefit formula rather than a conventional flat-percentage insured certificate.

## 02 - University of Texas System Voluntary STD Certificate

- **Organization:** The University of Texas System; issued by Dearborn Life Insurance Company and branded Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Texas

- **Plan/program design:** Employee-paid coverage. The certificate specifies 60% of weekly earnings up to $850 per week, a 7-day elimination period for injury and sickness, and a 22-week maximum payable period after the elimination period. ## 03 - New York Statutory Disability Statement of Rights

- **Title:** New York State Disability Benefits - Statement of Rights (DB-271S)
- **Organization:** New York State Workers' Compensation Board
- **Jurisdiction/market:** New York statutory disability benefits and Paid Family Leave interaction
- **Plan/program design:** Statutory non-occupational disability coverage beginning with the eighth consecutive day. The notice states 50% of average weekly wages, capped at $170 per week, for up to 26 weeks. Disability and New York Paid Family Leave cannot be taken simultaneously, and their combined duration in a 52-week period cannot exceed 26 weeks.
- **Document subtype:** One-page statutory rights notice and concise claim-filing guide
