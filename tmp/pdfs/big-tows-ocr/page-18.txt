Important things to know about
selecting your PCP

A primary care provider (PCP) is your health guide—someone who can help coordinate your care
and supports you in achieving your best health.

Your PCP:

* Must be a general practice, family practice,
pediatrician or internal medicine provider*

¢ Must be an individual provider, not a medical practice**

* Must be accepting new patients (if you are nota
current patient)

* Must be located in a town or city near where you
(the subscriber) live or work

* Can be selected for the entire family or each covered
member can select their own

A PCP is the doctor who knows you best—who understands your health history
and health goals. They’re who you turn to first—for everything from routine care to
prescriptions and more.

And, since most PCPs offer virtual visits for primary care, you can choose to see them
in person or from home.

So, whether or not your plan requires you to have a PCP, it’s a good idea to choose one.
