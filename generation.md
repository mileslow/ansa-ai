# Northstar synthetic booklet generation

This document records the requested Northstar Fabrication test case, the source
package that was created, how the product generated the booklet, and the code
changes required to produce a correct result.

## Requested outcome

The request was to create a complete, fictional input package for an employee
benefits booklet and then generate the booklet with Ansa's actual product
workflow.

The test case had to contain only these employee benefit choices:

- Medical: one Bronze, one Silver, and one Gold option.
- Dental: two options selected from the available dental plan inventory.
- No vision, Life/AD&D, disability, voluntary benefits, HSA program, HRA, FSA,
  telemedicine program, EAP, or other benefit offering.

The deliverables had to include:

1. A new synthetic version of the completed Northstar employer application.
2. A new synthetic Excel cost sheet aligned with the application and plans.
3. Complete plan-information fixtures for the five selected options.
4. All employer, eligibility, enrollment, contact, contribution, payroll, and
   deduction information needed for an employee booklet.
5. A final booklet generated through the real `employee_booklet` pipeline and
   the product's HTML-to-PDF renderer, rather than a separate booklet builder.
6. Clearly named files collected under `test-info/`.

Creating the synthetic application and source documents directly was approved.
The restriction was that the final booklet itself had to be generated by the
product.

## Selected plan package

The finalized synthetic offering is:

| Benefit | Selected options | Carrier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Medical | 2026 SimplyBlue Plus Bronze 4 | Excellus BlueCross BlueShield |
| Medical | 2026 SimplyBlue Plus Silver 19 | Excellus BlueCross BlueShield |
| Medical | 2026 SimplyBlue Plus Gold 6 | Excellus BlueCross BlueShield |
| Dental | 2026 Delta Dental Basic Family PPO Plan I | Delta Dental |
| Dental | 2026 Delta Dental Enhanced Family PPO Plan III | Delta Dental |

The application explicitly states that medical and dental are the only offered
benefit lines. Medical-plan HSA compatibility is treated only as a plan-design
characteristic; it does not create a standalone employer HSA offering.

## Synthetic employer and cost assumptions

The completed employer fixture contains the facts needed for booklet creation:

- Employer: Northstar Fabrication Test Company, DBA Northstar Fabrication.
- Plan year: January 1 through December 31, 2026.
- Open enrollment: November 3 through November 14, 2025.
- Eligibility: regular employees scheduled for at least 30 hours per week.
- Waiting period: first of the month after 30 calendar days of employment.
- Eligible dependents: spouse, domestic partner, and eligible children through
  the end of the month in which they turn age 26.
- Payroll frequency: biweekly, with 26 deductions per year.
- Enrollment method: the fictional Northstar Benefits Portal.
- Enrollment and HR contact information using reserved fictional contacts and
  `.test` domains.

The cost workbook uses these contribution rules:

- Medical: a flat $75 monthly employer contribution for every plan and tier.
- Basic dental: a flat $23 monthly employer credit for every tier, equal to 50%
  of its $46 employee-only premium.
- Enhanced dental: a flat $31.70 monthly employer credit for every tier, equal
  to 50% of its $63.40 employee-only premium.
- Employee monthly cost: monthly premium minus employer monthly contribution.
- Employee per-pay deduction: employee monthly cost multiplied by 12 and
  divided by 26.

The Excellus plan names, identifiers, designs, and base rates were aligned to
the local 2026 Q1 Excellus inventory. The dental option names and benefit
percentages were aligned to the local Delta Dental policy inventory. Employer
identity, contacts, dental premiums, contribution choices, and enrollment
counts are synthetic test data.

## Generation workflow

The package is produced by
`scripts/generate-northstar-medical-dental-test-package.ts`.

The script performs these operations:

1. Creates the completed employer application from paginated HTML with the
   product's `generateBookletPdfFromHtml` renderer.
2. Creates five synthetic, source-like plan summaries through the same HTML/PDF
   renderer.
3. Creates a formula-driven `.xlsx` workbook with assumptions, medical costs,
   dental costs, cached formula results, enrollment counts, and an annual
   summary.
4. Loads the seven generated inputs as `LoadedUploadedFile` records.
5. Calls `runBookletPipeline` with `enforceRegistry: false`, which is the
   product's `employee_booklet` behavior.
6. Runs document classification, employer and plan extraction, detailed
   medical parsing, deterministic rate-sheet parsing, plan-to-rate matching,
   offered-benefit detection, conflict and blocker checks, outline generation,
   source-backed HTML assembly, preflight checks, PDF rendering, and post-render
   quality checks.
7. Writes the final PDF, HTML, and pipeline audit metadata to
   `test-info/04_generated-booklet/`.

The application supplies the exact employer name. A conservative extraction
pass also found a shortened `Northstar` reference in plan-document contact
language, so the run records `Northstar Fabrication` as the accountable answer
to that one naming conflict. The run also explicitly confirms that no employer
HSA benefit is offered. Both answers repeat facts already stated in the
application; they do not add a new benefit or unsupported plan fact.

Run the fixture from the repository root with:

```bash
npx tsx scripts/generate-northstar-medical-dental-test-package.ts
```

The live extraction phases require `OPENAI_API_KEY` in the local environment.
The renderer also requires the project's supported Chromium installation.

## Code changes required

### Package generator

`scripts/generate-northstar-medical-dental-test-package.ts` was added to make
the test repeatable. It owns only synthetic fixture construction and
orchestration. It does not contain a parallel final-booklet implementation; the
final output is returned by `runBookletPipeline`.

### Separate medical and dental carriers

Visual QA exposed an existing adapter problem. The normalized package contained
the correct carrier on every plan, but the legacy renderer adapter mapped both
medical and dental to one `medicalDental` property. Since dental appeared later
in the plan list, `Delta Dental` overwrote the medical carrier and appeared on
the Excellus medical pages.

The correction required two production changes:

- `lib/booklet-package-adapter.ts` now preserves separate `medical` and
  `dental` carrier entries. It also retains a `medicalDental` fallback for older
  company-shaped fixtures.
- `lib/booklet.ts` now reads the benefit-specific carrier first on medical and
  dental pages, then falls back to the legacy combined property.

This was a general product fix, not a Northstar-specific hardcoded override.

### Regression coverage

`tests/booklet-package-adapter.test.ts` now verifies both of these cases:

- medical and dental using the same carrier remain supported;
- medical and dental using different carriers remain separate through the
  adapter.

The focused adapter and core-section test run passes 48 tests.
